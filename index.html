i'm a first year phd student at mit's brain and cognitive sciences, working with josh tenenbaum in the computational cognitive science group. at a high level, i'm interested in studying cognitive representations and abstractions for planning - how people quickly learn and construct models of the world around them, and the computational mechanisms that allow these world models to balance the tradeoff between efficiency, flexibility, and fidelity. i'm particularly interested in studying this question in multi-agent contexts, where people's representations of the environment and other agents can often interact in complex ways. i love to listen to other people's music - so if you have a song you'd like to recommend, i'd love to hear it :)

previously i was a student and postbac at boston college, where i studied psychology, mathematics, and computer science (minor), and worked with joshua hartshorne, liane young, and stefano anzellotti.
