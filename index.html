i'm a first year phd student at mit's brain and cognitive sciences, working with josh tenenbaum in the computational cognitive science group. at a high level, i'm primarily interested in how people learn and exploit efficient representations and abstractions for planning and search. i'd like to study these questions in both single and multi-agent environments, in an attempt to bridge key ideas from the planning and ai, with social cognition and game theory. to do so, i draw on a computational toolkit with methods from program synthesis, robotics, and bayesian statistics / probabilistic programming.
previously i was a student and postbac at boston college, where i studied psychology, mathematics, and computer science (minor), and worked with joshua hartshorne, liane young, and stefano anzellotti.
